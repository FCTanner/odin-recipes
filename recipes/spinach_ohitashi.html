Spinach Ohitashi

Description

Spinach Ohitashi is a classic Japanese side dish that highlights the natural flavor of spinach through a simple and elegant preparation. The spinach is lightly blanched, then marinated in a soy-based dashi sauce that infuses it with umami. Garnished with sesame seeds or grated ginger, this dish is light, healthy, and perfect as a starter or accompaniment to a larger Japanese meal.

Ingredients

  • 200g fresh spinach
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons dashi stock (can be made with instant dashi powder)
  • 1 teaspoon mirin (optional for a touch of sweetness)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds, toasted
  • A pinch of grated ginger (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Spinach:
    • Wash the spinach thoroughly and trim the ends.
    • Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
    • Blanch the spinach for 30-60 seconds, or until it turns bright green.
    • Immediately transfer the spinach to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking and preserve the vibrant color.
  2. Drain and Shape the Spinach:
    • Once cooled, gently squeeze out excess water from the spinach.
    • Form the spinach into small bundles and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Sauce:
    • In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, dashi stock, mirin (if using), and sugar (if using). Adjust to taste.
  4. Marinate the Spinach:
    • Arrange the spinach bundles in a shallow dish.
    • Pour the soy-dashi mixture over the spinach and let it marinate for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Serve:
    • Transfer the spinach bundles to a serving plate.
    • Drizzle a small amount of the marinade over the top.
    • Sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds and garnish with grated ginger if desired.
